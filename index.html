Busy but Blind: Losing Depth in the Age of Instant Answers

I’ve been noticing a pattern in how we work today. We want to do more, faster, and we’ve built tools to make it possible. The problem is, the faster we go, the less we think.

The Lost Art of Focus

There was a time when solving a hard problem meant sitting with it for hours, wrestling with it until an idea emerged. Now answers arrive instantly. Summaries appear before we’ve read the full report. Suggestions pop up before we’ve formed our own opinion.

Deep work is slow. It asks for patience. It rewards those who stay.

Deep work requires patience. Without it, we end up busy, productive, and creatively bankrupt.

Interruptions Everywhere

I’ve seen it in teams, in meetings, even in myself. We check one task and jump to the next. Interruptions pile up. We feel efficient, but the work that matters most never gets the space it needs.

What We Can Do

Protect thinking time. Make space for questions that don’t have immediate answers. Sit with problems instead of skipping straight to conclusions.

Speed without thought is just motion.

Some things have to happen in the human mind before anything else…
